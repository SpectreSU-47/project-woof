Bourgeois Beagles!

Their noses are truly the arbitrator in their life, guiding them day by day to an ever so interesting scent. The Beagle originally was bred as a scenthound to track small game, mostly rabbits and bunnies alike.

    The magnificent stride of the breed is quite rare among most, as you can tell by the Beagle puppy, prancing through a field gracefully.

    This is a Beagle that is standing in a on-guard type of position, it is presumably an adolescent or adult.

    Here is another younger Beagle chewing on a bone. This distinctive posture is an example of their more primitive nature.

The Beagle is quite the interesting dog, while not my personal favorite, they do hold an interesting physique. Some examples of such characteristics would be: small, compact, and hardy. Canines in this dog breed are merry and fun loving, but being hounds, they can also be contrary and require patient and somewhat creative training techniques.
